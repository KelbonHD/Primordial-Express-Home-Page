Primordial Express

Traditional, Fantastic Meals

About Us

We here at Primordial Express are a family run business who strive to provide our customers with interesting, unique, and delicious dishes that come from four different culture's traditional time periods.

We do all of this all while providing fast service and fair prices. I hope you enjoy your time here at Primordial Express!

-Frenken Staunchhammer, Founder of Primordial Express

Our 4 Food Menus

Primordial Express has four menus for you to find what you'd like to eat. Take a look below, and get ready to get hungry!
Each dish comes with one side. You can choose different sides from different menus if you'd like!

Traditional English Menu

Shepard's Pie

An absolute staple in traditional English cuisine! A meat pie consisting of lamb and vegetables cooked in our homemade gravy, topped with savory mashed potatoes.
$9.99 (Add Cheese +$1)

Full English Breakfast

No English menu is complete without their traditional breakfast dish! A full platter of bacon, sausage, eggs, beans, mushrooms, tomatoes, and your choice of toast.
$12.99

Lancashire Hot Pot

Another great dish once again influenced by potatoes! A casserole consisting of lamb, onion and carrots cooked in chicken stock, with thin sliced potatoes served over.
$10.99 (Add Cheese +$1)

Toad in the Hole

A classic dish following the usual english simplicity. Pork sausages cooked then baked into a soft yorkshire style batter, and served with gravy on the side or on top.
$8.99

English Sides:

  • Yorkshire Pudding

  • Cauliflower Cheese

Traditional Mongolian Menu

Khorkhog

One of if not the most popular and favorited among traditional mongolian dishes! We take our most tender sheep or lamb meat and mix it with carrots, onions, potatoes and cabbage in a giant pot, and cook it with hot stones.
$14.99

Huushuur

Huushuur are very delicious meat pies popular in Mongolian Festivals. Fried pastries filled with your choice of beef or sheep and onions (5 ct.), served with gravy, and a sample of dumplings and steam vegetables.
$13.99

Tsuivan

A very filling and satisfying stir fried noodle dish that carries that similar Mongolian food style! Noodles cut homemade and stir fried with steamed potatoes, carrots, and onions and your choice of beef and sheep.
$13.99

Chanasan Makh and Bin

A simple yet effectively great Mongolian dish. The fattiest cuts of your choice of beef, sheep, or goat boiled with potatoes, carrots, and cabbage, and topped off with salt. Served with a side of Bin, a traditional Mongolian recipe for fried bread, and a dill inspired sour sauce.
$14.99

Mongolian Sides

  • Buuz or Khuushuur Dumplings
  • Guriltai Shul (Mongolian Meat and Noodle Soup)
  • Yak Yogurt
  • Budaatai Huurga (Stir Fried Rice)

Traditional Indian Menu

Tandoori Chicken

A flavor packed traditional Indian recipe. Chicken legs/drums skewered and cooked in a clay oven with a special marinade sauce consisting of yogurt, tandoori masala, cumin, and nutmeg.
$9.99

Masala Dosa

A popular dish in the Southern regions of India. Consists of Masala, potatoes cooked with onions and mustard seeds, served in Dosa, a thin flatbread made from fermented rice and black gram beans (3 ct.)
$11.99

Korma

A hearty and tasty stew served in a fair sized bowl. The stew consists of beef and/or chicken, cooked in a tangy mixture of yogurt, saffron, and various other spices (consult waiter/waitress for more details)
$9.99

Idli

A simple, yet filling start to anyones day. Fermented rice and lentil cakes filled with your choice of beef or chicken, then steamed (6 ct.). Served with tangy red and saffron sauce.
$7.99

Indian Sides

  • Biryani (Indian Spiced Rice)

  • Dal Makhani (Indian Bean Soup)

  • Palak Paneer (Indian Cheese Spinach Puree)

  • Samosa
    (Indian Pastries with choice of vegetable or meat filling)

Traditional Japanese Menu

Yakisoba

A popular Japanese traditional dish among festivals. Noodles stir fried in a sweet and savory sauce alongside your choice of protein (chicken, pork, beef, or shrimp), diced onions, and various spices (consult waiter/waitress for more detail)
$8.99

Tonkatsu

A western twist on Japanese cuisine. A crispy-fried pork cutlet served with gravy, mashed potatoes, and crispy cabbage.
$11.99

Tempura

Tempura is a simple, yet tasty classic. Your choice of shrimp, chicken, vegetables or tofu deep fried in our homemade Tempura batter, and served with soy sauce.
$9.99

Fluffy Japanese Pancakes

A deliciously sweet plate of pancakes for those in the breakfast mindset. Comes with 3 tall and fluffy pancakes... and syrup ofcourse!
$8.99

Traditional Desserts

Perhaps you'd like something on the sweeter side, or maybe want some more calories to wash down those other calories. Well look no further than our dessert menu, with lots of delicious choices!

Trifle

A sweet treat coming for the traditional English cookbook. A layer of cake topped with cream, blueberries and strawberries served in a glass.
$5.99

Boortsog

A sweet fried dessert originating from Mongolia. Flour based cookies fried in oil and topped with salt and sugar. Served with creamy jam or creamy butter.(8 ct.)
$5.99

Gulab Jamun

A delectable Indian dessert that's sweet and smooth. Plum sized milk powder dough balls fried in a lemony oil and covered in a rose water flavored syrup.(10 ct.)
$5.99

Purin

A traditional japanese and french dessert. A milk custard served in a cup topped with melted sugar syrup.
$2.99

Daifuku

Also known as Daifuku Mochi, a popular japanese dessert consisting of a thin rice cake filled with red bean paste, with your choice of chestnut, strawberry, or apricot fruit insert. (4 ct.)
$5.99

Pistachio Chocolate Banana Sushi

A strange and abnormal dessert to finish off our list! A whole banana covered melted chocolate, frozen, chopped up then topped off with ground up pistachios.
$5.99
